 Wednesday: Minutes from the Fed's January policy meeting should provide new clues into how officials judged risks to the economy when they decided to remove language from their policy statement that had previously shown a bias toward raising rates. The written account of the meeting could also illustrate how officials are approaching plans to complete the runoff of their $4 trillion portfolio of bonds and other assets that they initiated in 2017.
Thursday: The Commerce Department releases December durable-goods data. In November, demand for long-lasting goods produced by U.S. factories rose because of ramped-up aircraft orders. Meanwhile, an underlying business-investment gauge, new orders for nondefense capital goods excluding aircraft, fell 0.6% from October. Economists surveyed by The Wall Street Journal forecast durable-goods orders rose 1.5% in December.
The National Association of Realtors releases January existing-home sales data. Home sales tumbled in December to their weakest level since 2015, ending a weak year for the housing market despite a strong economy. Economists surveyed by The Wall Street Journal forecast existing home sales rose 2.2% to a 5.10 million annual rate in January.
